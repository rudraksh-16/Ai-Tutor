
The formal birth of Artificial Intelligence occurred in the 1950s. In 1950, Alan Turing proposed the Turing Test, a method to evaluate whether a machine could exhibit intelligent behavior comparable to that of a human.

In 1956, the Dartmouth Conference officially launched Artificial Intelligence as a research discipline. Organized by John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, Claude Shannon, and others, the term “Artificial Intelligence” was coined during this event.

Early AI research focused on symbolic reasoning and problem-solving. Programs such as the Logic Theorist and the General Problem Solver demonstrated that machines could solve structured problems and prove mathematical theorems.

### Characteristics of Early AI
- Rule-based logic
- Symbolic reasoning
- Strong optimism about rapid progress
- Limited computing resources

### Conclusion
This era laid the academic foundation of AI, even though many early predictions later proved overly optimistic.
